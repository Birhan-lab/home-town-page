About Addis Ababa

Addis Ababa is the largest city in Ethiopia, with a population of over 5 million. It is the political, economic, and cultural center of the country and home to the African Union's headquarters.

Founded in 1886 by Emperor Menelik II, the city lies at the foot of the Entoto Mountains, offering a mix of modern urban life and historic landmarks.

Famous Landmarks

  • National Museum of EthiopiaHome to the famous fossil 'Lucy'.
  • Holy Trinity CathedralThe most important religious building in Ethiopia.
  • Mount EntotoOffers a panoramic view of the city.
  • Meskel SquareThe heart of public gatherings and celebrations.

Cultural Highlights

Addis Ababa is known for its vibrant cultural life, including music, arts, and traditional coffee ceremonies. The city's markets, such as Mercato, are bustling with activity, offering a wide range of Ethiopian goods, including spices, crafts, and more.
